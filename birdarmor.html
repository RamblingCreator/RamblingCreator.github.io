Bird Armor

I made this for a costume design class that I took during my senior year of high school. The assignment was to design and create a costume for a stuffed animal.

❮ ❯

Process

Planning

Ideas

My plan was to make armor for a stuffed kiwi, and I decided that I wanted to make a leather helmet, gambeson-like jacket, and pants. These are the images that i used as inspiration for the design. I knew that I wanted the pants to be black, and that the helmet would be brown, but I didnâ€™t decide the color of the jacket until I was looking at the fabric options.

Planning

It was difficult to create the pattern. I had to create a pair of pants for something that was always sitting down, with strange proportions. I also had to figure out how to make a jacket for something without arms, but that wasn't quite as difficult.

❮ ❯

Jacket

Patterning

This is the pattern for the jacket. I used it to draft the lines for the quilting on the final piece.
I cut out 2 of this piece from green fabric, and 1 from a thicker fabric

Sewing

First, I sewed the pieces into a pocket, so that the thicker piece would be on the inside when it was turned inside out Next, I closed the final seam of the pocket so that I had 1 piece of material with the thicker part sandwiched between the green pieces.

Finishing

I drew evenly spaced diagonal lines to guide where I was going to sew to quilt it. Then, I sewed along all these lines to create the quilted effect, then trimmed the loose threads from the ends. Last, I attached a hook and eye closure to the jacket so that it would be wearable.

❮ ❯

Pants

Patterning

This is the pattern for the pants. The crumpled versions are the ones that I pinned to the stuffed animal so that I could test it. I cut 2 of each piece out.

Sewing

This picture is from when I was partway through sewing the pants pieces together. I had hemmed some of the edges and sewn together the outside seam on one half of the pants.

Finishing

After finishing the outside seams, I sewed the 2 halves to each other and hemmed the final edge. Next, I sewed the top leg seams. This picture was taken at this point. I noticed that the waistband stuck really far out to the back, so I also folded and sewed the back seam in so it would fit better.

❮ ❯

Helmet

Patterning

The first step was drafting the pattern. I made 5 total pieces: the beak, main head section, and 3 back segments. I cut these pieces out of leather, then punched the holes for riveting and sewing.

Finishing

I used a piece of cardboard to form the beak into a curved shape. Finally, I dyed the pieces using brown dye, then sewed & riveted them together. I needed to sew the beak to the main section and rivet the back pieces to the main section.
